# Key Insights
- **Account Farming**: Daniel advocates buying established Reddit accounts (with karma) from third-party sites and using anti-detect browser software (like AdsPower) to hide multiple identities from Reddit’s moderation systems.
- **The "Artificial Consensus" Tactic:** Daniel suggests using secondary accounts to ask "What tool did you use?" under your own primary account's post, then replying with your product link to simulate a natural recommendation.
- **Technical Evasion**: Advice focuses on avoiding "autobans" by using mobile hotspots to rotate IP addresses, staggering account switches by 15 minutes, and waiting hours before adding links to posts to evade automated filters.

# My Takeaways for Reddit B2B SaaS Strategy
- **Warm Leads vs. Cold Ads**: Reddit traffic is higher intent than paid ads because users clicking links are typically already searching for a solution to the problem your tool solves.
- **Rotation is Mandatory**: Never post in the same subreddit more than once a week. Rotate between 5–7 niche-specific subreddits to maintain an "organic" look.
- "**Doxxing" Risk**: If Reddit’s automated systems or human moderators link our multiple accounts to one origin, they will issue an IP-wide "shadowban" across all accounts, effectively blacklisting our domain from Reddit permanently.
