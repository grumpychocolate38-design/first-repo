## Post Content

Reddit isn’t the AEO playbook everyone is selling you.

I keep seeing the same data point recycled: Reddit threads are frequently cited by LLMs, and therefore, you need a Reddit strategy. I get the appeal of that narrative, but the actual data is either shallow or covers millions of cited URLs, not a B2B-specific dataset.

When you dig into the threads that are getting cited, many of them are years old. Some are from six or seven years ago, with a handful of upvotes and no real consensus. The LLMs pulled these threads from Reddit because the corpus was wide and deep, and not because the content was good or authoritative. 

This is the same mistake I see teams make with SEO, content, and GTM advice. Someone publishes a study that says "here's what works," and suddenly everyone applies it uniformly without asking whether it applies to their industry, their unique audience, or their buyer's intent. It's the equivalent of saying "here's how to rank every blog post" without understanding the search landscape for that specific term.

If you're building an AEO strategy, you need to understand what the LLM is actually pulling from and citing, and why. And much like you would need to understand the specific search intent behind a query for organic search, you need to understand the specific citation sources for each prompt or topic. 

A blanket "go post on Reddit" strategy is just the 2026 version of what "go build more backlinks" was in 2016. And for most brands, it won’t be good advice, especially since Reddit users are eagle-eyed and often resistant to content from brands. 

I'd rather see teams spend that energy making their own content more specific, useful, and clearly tied to the problems their buyers are actually asking about. That's what gets cited long-term, and it’s what matters to your customers.

## Key Insights
- **The "Backlink" Trap**: A blanket "post on Reddit" strategy is outdated, mirroring the ineffective "build more backlinks" mentality of 2016. It ignores that Reddit users are highly resistant to brand-led content and will likely reject generic efforts.
- **Context Over Correlation**: Just because LLMs pull data from Reddit doesn't mean Reddit is a universal solution for every brand. Strategies must be tailored to your specific industry, buyer intent, and competitive landscape.
- **Quality Outperforms Reach**: AI citation isn't just about presence; it's about the utility and specificity of the information. Content that directly and uniquely solves buyer problems is more likely to be cited long-term than generic community engagement.

## My Takeaways for Reddit B2B SaaS Strategy
- **Prioritize Specificity and Utility**: Redirect energy from forced Reddit community participation toward creating highly specific, useful content that addresses the actual, nuanced problems your buyers are searching for.
- **Conduct Deep Citation Analysis**: Before investing in Reddit, investigate what your target LLMs are actually citing for your specific industry topics. Understand the "why" behind those citations rather than assuming a broad Reddit strategy will work.
- **Reject "Uniform" Advice**: Avoid applying one-size-fits-all growth hacking tactics. Evaluate whether Reddit truly aligns with your unique audience and buyer intent, rather than following the current industry trend.
